Chicken Broccoli Alfredo Pasta

Notes

  • Yield: 4 servings
  • Prep: 15 min | Cook: 30 min | Total: 45 min

Ingredients

  • 10 oz broccoli
  • 8 oz penne pasta (for gluten-free, use gluten-free brown rice penne)
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1.5 lb chicken breast (about 1 large chicken breast)
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup milk
  • 1 1/4 cup Parmesan, Mozzarella, or Monterey Jack cheese, shredded
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions

  1. Cook broccoli in boiling water for 5 minutes; drain well and cut into small florets. Cook pasta al dente according to package directions; drain.
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil and butter on medium-high heat (until hot but not smoking). Saut√© chicken breast (seasoned generously with salt and pepper on both sides) for about 5 minutes on each side.
  3. Remove the skillet from heat, cover with the lid, and let the chicken rest off heat until it is no longer pink in the center and completely cooked through. Remove chicken from the pan and cut into bite-size pieces.
  4. Add minced garlic to the same skillet and cook it in the chicken juices and oil remaining in the pan, scraping up the browned bits from the bottom, on medium heat for about 30 seconds or until fragrant. Add cooked broccoli and mix with the garlic. Remove from the pan.
  5. To make the creamy sauce, in the same skillet heat 1 cup of heavy cream until it just boils. Immediately add the cheese and stir until melted and creamy.
  6. Reduce heat to medium-low and slowly add the milk in small amounts, stirring until the sauce reaches the desired consistency.
  7. Add the cooked pasta, broccoli, and chicken back into the skillet and toss to coat with the sauce. Season with additional salt and pepper if needed and serve warm.
