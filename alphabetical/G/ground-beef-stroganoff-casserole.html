Ground Beef Stroganoff Casserole

Notes

  • Prep: 30 min
  • Cook: 40 min
  • Total: 1 hr 10 min

Main

  • 1 1/2 lbs ground beef
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 lb mushrooms, washed, drained, and cut into quarters (brown Crimini mushrooms recommended)
  • 1 onion, chopped small
  • 4 cups cauliflower rice (see notes)
  • 1 tsp steak seasoning
  • Salt and fresh-ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup grated mozzarella (for topping)

Sauce

  • 1 tsp Better Than Bouillon Beef Base (or 1 beef bouillon cube)
  • 1/4 cup water (to reduce with bouillon)
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 cup mayo
  • 1 cup grated mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 tsp steak seasoning

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F. Spray a large glass or ceramic casserole dish with non-stick spray or brush with a little olive oil.
  2. Mix the Better Than Bouillon Beef Base (or bouillon cube) with 1/4 cup water in a very small pan. Simmer until reduced to 1 tbsp of thick stock. Let cool.
  3. Heat a couple of teaspoons of olive oil in a large heavy frying pan. Cook the ground beef over medium-high heat until completely browned, breaking it apart as it cooks. Season with 1 tsp steak seasoning, salt, and pepper to taste.
  4. Transfer the browned beef to the prepared casserole dish.
  5. While the beef is browning, wash the mushrooms and spin dry in a salad spinner or drain well. Chop the cauliflower and pulse in a food processor to make cauliflower rice, if not using pre-riced cauliflower.
  6. In the same pan, heat remaining olive oil if needed. Add mushrooms and onion and sauté until the mushrooms release their liquid and the onions are softened and lightly browned.
  7. Add the cauliflower rice to the pan and cook, stirring, until just tender and any excess moisture has cooked off. Season lightly with salt and pepper.
  8. Spread the mushroom, onion, and cauliflower mixture evenly over the ground beef in the casserole dish.
  9. In a mixing bowl, combine the reduced beef stock, sour cream, mayo, 1 cup mozzarella, Parmesan, and 1/2 tsp steak seasoning. Stir until smooth.
  10. Spread the sauce mixture evenly over the top of the casserole. Sprinkle with the remaining 1 cup mozzarella cheese.
  11. Bake for about 40 minutes, or until the casserole is bubbling and the top is lightly browned.
  12. Let cool for a few minutes before serving.
