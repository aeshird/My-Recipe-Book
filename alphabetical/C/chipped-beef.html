Chipped Beef

Ingredients

  • 3–4 tbsp margarine or butter
  • 2–3 tbsp flour (enough to form a paste)
  • Approximately 1/2 lb chipped beef (such as Carl Buddig beef), chopped
  • Milk, as needed
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Toast, for serving

Instructions

  1. Melt margarine or butter in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
  2. Stir in flour a little at a time until a smooth paste forms.
  3. Add chopped beef and cook until the edges are browned and the meat is heated through. The butter will mostly be absorbed.
  4. Add milk a little at a time, stirring constantly, until the mixture reaches the desired creamy consistency.
  5. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Serve hot over toast.

Notes

  • You can spray the pan first and brown the meat before making the roux.
  • Alternatively, remove the browned meat, then make the sauce with butter, milk, and flour to desired thickness, and add the meat back in to heat through.
