The Best Beef Stew

Notes

  • Yield: 6 servings
  • Prep Time: 30 minutes
  • Cook Time: 2 hours

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 lbs beef stew meat, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 1/2 tsp salt, divided
  • 6 tbsp all-purpose flour, divided
  • 1/2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tbsp canola oil
  • 3 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 tsp herbes de Provence
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 cups dry red wine
  • 2 cups beef broth
  • 1 1/2 tsp minced fresh rosemary, divided
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 3 cups cubed peeled potatoes
  • 3 cups coarsely chopped onions
  • 2 cups sliced carrots
  • 2 tbsp cold water
  • 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1 cup fresh or frozen peas

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, toss the beef with 1/4 tsp of the salt. In a large bowl, combine 4 tbsp of the flour and the smoked paprika. Add the beef, a few pieces at a time, and toss to coat.
  2. Heat the canola oil in a Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the floured beef and brown on all sides.
  3. Stir in the tomato paste, herbes de Provence, and minced garlic. Cook for 1–2 minutes, until fragrant and slightly darkened.
  4. Pour in the red wine and bring to a gentle boil. Reduce the heat and simmer until the liquid is reduced by half, about 5 minutes.
  5. Stir in the beef broth, 1 tsp of the rosemary, bay leaves, and the remaining 1/4 tsp salt. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat, cover, and simmer until the meat is almost tender, about 1 1/2 hours.
  6. Add the potatoes, onions, and carrots. Cover and simmer until the vegetables and meat are tender, about 30 minutes.
  7. In a small bowl, whisk the remaining 2 tbsp flour with the 2 tbsp cold water until smooth. Stir this slurry into the stew and bring to a gentle boil. Cook, stirring, for 2–3 minutes until slightly thickened.
  8. Stir in the balsamic vinegar, peas, and remaining 1/2 tsp rosemary. Heat through for a few minutes. Remove and discard the bay leaves before serving.
