One Pot Parmesan Pasta

Notes

  • Yield: 6 servings
  • Cook: 15 min

Ingredients

  • 4 1/2 cups low-sodium chicken stock
  • 12 oz spaghetti pasta, uncooked
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 can (14.5 oz) Rotel or petite diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup fresh basil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Juice and zest of 1 lemon
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/2 tbsp sea salt
  • 1 tsp fresh ground black pepper
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup shredded Parmesan cheese
  • 1 cup shaved Parmesan cheese
  • Additional fresh basil, for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a deep skillet or heavy-bottomed pot, add the uncooked spaghetti, tomatoes, onions, basil, garlic, lemon juice and zest. Drizzle with olive oil, then add the salt and pepper. Pour in the chicken stock and stir everything together.
  2. On medium-high heat, bring the mixture to a boil. Cover and cook for 8 to 10 minutes. Halfway through cooking, give it a good stir to make sure the pasta does not stick together. Cover and cook for an additional 2 to 3 minutes, or until the pasta is cooked.
  3. Stir in the heavy cream and both Parmesan cheeses. Let the pasta sit for a few minutes so the sauce can thicken up, then garnish with more basil and serve.
