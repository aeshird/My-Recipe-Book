One-Pan Cheesy Chicken, Broccoli, and Rice

Notes

  • Prep: 5 min
  • Cook: 25 min
  • Total: 30 min

Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp extra virgin olive oil, divided
  • 1/2 medium yellow onion, finely diced
  • 1 lb boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup uncooked extra-long grain white rice
  • 2 1/2 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 2 1/2 cups broccoli florets
  • 2 cups shredded extra-sharp cheddar cheese

Instructions

  1. In a large skillet or pan, sauté the onions in 2 tbsp olive oil over medium heat. Season the chicken with salt and pepper.
  2. Once the onions soften, increase the heat to medium-high and add the chicken to the pan. Brown the chicken pieces, then add the garlic and cook for about 1 more minute.
  3. Push the chicken to one side of the pan and add the remaining 1 tbsp olive oil to the other side. Add the uncooked rice to the oil and sauté it for a couple of minutes.
  4. Add the chicken broth to the pan and bring the mixture to a boil. Lower the heat to a simmer and cover the pan with a lid.
  5. Cook the chicken and rice mixture, covered, for about 12 minutes. Sprinkle the broccoli evenly over the chicken and rice mixture and stir to combine. Continue to cook, covered, for another 8 minutes on low, or until both the broccoli and rice are tender.
  6. Remove from heat and stir in 1/2 cup of the cheese. Sprinkle the remaining cheese on top and cover with a lid, letting it sit for 1 to 2 minutes or until the cheese has melted. Serve warm.
