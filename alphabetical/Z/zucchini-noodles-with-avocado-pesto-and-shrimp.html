Zucchini Noodles with Avocado Pesto & Shrimp

Notes

  • Yield: 4 servings
  • Prep Time: 35 minutes
  • Total Time: 35 minutes

Ingredients

  • 5–6 medium zucchini (2 1/4–2 1/2 pounds total), trimmed
  • 3/4 tsp salt, divided
  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 1 cup packed fresh basil leaves
  • 1/4 cup unsalted shelled pistachios
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/4 tsp ground pepper
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil, plus 2 tbsp, divided
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 pound raw shrimp (21–25 count), peeled and deveined, tails left on if desired
  • 1–2 tsp Old Bay seasoning

Instructions

  1. Using a spiral vegetable slicer or a vegetable peeler, cut the zucchini lengthwise into long, thin strands or strips. Stop when you reach the seeds in the middle, as the seeds make the noodles fall apart.
  2. Place the zucchini noodles in a colander and toss with 1/2 tsp salt. Let drain for 15–30 minutes, then gently squeeze to remove any excess water.
  3. Meanwhile, combine the avocado, basil, pistachios, lemon juice, pepper, and the remaining 1/4 tsp salt in a food processor. Pulse until finely chopped. Add 1/4 cup olive oil and process until smooth to make the pesto. Set aside.
  4. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add 1 clove minced garlic and cook for about 30 seconds, until fragrant.
  5. Add the shrimp, sprinkle with Old Bay seasoning, and cook, stirring often, until the shrimp are pink and cooked through, about 3–4 minutes. Transfer the shrimp to a plate and keep warm.
  6. In the same skillet, add the remaining 1 tbsp olive oil and the remaining minced garlic. Cook for 30 seconds, then add the zucchini noodles. Toss and cook for 2–3 minutes, just until warmed and slightly tender.
  7. Remove the skillet from the heat and add the avocado pesto to the zucchini noodles. Toss until the noodles are evenly coated.
  8. Divide the zucchini noodles among plates and top with the cooked shrimp. Serve immediately.
