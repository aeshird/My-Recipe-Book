Zucchini Lasagna Rolls with Smoked Mozzarella

Notes

  • Yield: 4 servings
  • Prep Time: 30 minutes
  • Total Time: 1 hour

Ingredients

  • 2 large zucchini, trimmed
  • 2 tsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp ground pepper, divided
  • 1/4 tsp salt, divided
  • 8 tbsp shredded smoked mozzarella cheese, divided
  • 3 tbsp grated Parmesan cheese, divided
  • 1 large egg, lightly beaten
  • 1 1/3 cups part-skim ricotta
  • 1 (10 oz) package frozen spinach, thawed and squeezed dry
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 3/4 cup low-sodium marinara sauce, divided
  • 2 tbsp chopped fresh basil

Instructions

  1. Position racks in the upper and lower thirds of the oven; preheat to 425°F (220°C). Coat 2 rimmed baking sheets with cooking spray.
  2. Slice the zucchini lengthwise into 24 strips, about 1/8 inch thick each.
  3. In a large bowl, toss the zucchini strips with the olive oil, 1/4 tsp pepper, and 1/8 tsp salt. Arrange the zucchini in single layers on the prepared baking sheets.
  4. Bake the zucchini, turning once halfway through, until tender, about 10 minutes total. Remove from the oven and let cool slightly.
  5. Meanwhile, in a small bowl, combine 2 tbsp shredded smoked mozzarella and 1 tbsp Parmesan cheese. Set aside for topping.
  6. In a medium bowl, stir together the egg, ricotta, spinach, garlic, the remaining 6 tbsp smoked mozzarella, the remaining 2 tbsp Parmesan, and the remaining 1/4 tsp each salt and pepper until well combined.
  7. Spread 1/4 cup marinara sauce in the bottom of a baking dish.
  8. Working with one zucchini strip at a time, spread about 1 heaping tablespoon of the ricotta mixture over the strip, then roll up from one end. Place the rolls seam-side down in the prepared baking dish. Repeat with the remaining zucchini and filling.
  9. Spoon the remaining 1/2 cup marinara sauce over the rolls. Sprinkle with the reserved mozzarella-Parmesan mixture.
  10. Bake until the cheese is melted and bubbly and the filling is heated through, about 15â€“20 minutes.
  11. Sprinkle with chopped fresh basil before serving.
