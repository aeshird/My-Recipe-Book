Beef with Broccoli

Ingredients

Rice

  • 2 cups uncooked instant brown rice
  • 1 3/4 cups water

Sauce

  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp packed brown sugar

Main

  • 1 tbsp olive or vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp freshly grated ginger root
  • 1 lb boneless beef round or tip steak, thinly sliced
  • 1 small onion, thinly sliced (about 1/2 cup)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1/2 lb fresh broccoli florets and peeled stems, cut into bite-size pieces (about 2 cups)

Instructions

  1. Cook the rice in the water as directed on the package.
  2. In a small bowl, mix the beef broth, cornstarch, soy sauce, and brown sugar. Stir until the cornstarch is dissolved.
  3. In a 12-inch nonstick skillet, heat the oil over medium-high heat. Add the ginger, beef, and onion; sprinkle with salt and pepper. Cook 2–4 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the beef is browned. Remove the beef mixture from the skillet and cover to keep warm.
  4. Add the broth mixture and broccoli to the skillet. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat. Cook 5–10 minutes or until the sauce thickens and the broccoli is tender.
  5. Return the beef mixture to the skillet and cook until heated through, stirring occasionally. Serve over warm rice.

Notes

  • You can substitute white rice.
  • Can also substitute 1 lb pork tenderloin (thinly sliced) for the beef and 1 cup chicken broth for the beef broth.
