Breakfast Fried Brown Rice

Notes

  • Yield: 2 servings
  • Total: ~35 min

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cooked brown rice (preferably leftover from day before)
  • 6 slices thick-cut bacon, chopped
  • 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1–2 tsp low-sodium soy sauce
  • 4 green onions, sliced
  • 2 tsp toasted sesame oil

For Serving

  • 2 eggs, cooked as desired
  • 1/2 avocado, sliced
  • 1 tbsp freshly snipped chives
  • Hot sauce, to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat a large skillet or wok over low heat and add the bacon. Cook until the fat is rendered and the bacon is crispy. Remove the bacon with a slotted spoon and place on a paper towel–lined plate. Pour the bacon fat into a bowl, leaving about 1 tbsp in the skillet.
  2. Increase heat to medium-low. Add the lightly beaten eggs and stir constantly with a wooden spoon until just scrambled. Remove the eggs and set aside.
  3. Add the remaining bacon fat back to the skillet and increase heat to high. Add the rice, tossing to coat, then let it sit 1–2 minutes to crisp and brown slightly. Stir, break up clumps, and repeat 1–2 more times until rice is a bit crispy and golden. Season with soy sauce to taste.
  4. Add the bacon and scrambled eggs back into the skillet along with the green onions. Stir well and reduce heat to low while you cook the eggs for topping.
  5. Cook the topping eggs as desired and season with salt, pepper, and crushed red pepper if liked.
  6. To serve, spoon fried rice into bowls and top with an egg, sliced avocado, and a sprinkle of chives. Add hot sauce if desired.
