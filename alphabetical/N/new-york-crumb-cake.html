New York Crumb Cake

Notes

  • Yield: 12 servings
  • Prep: 30 min | Cook: 40 min | Total: 1 hr 10 min

Crumb Topping

  • 1/3 cup granulated sugar (2 2/3 oz)
  • 1/3 cup dark brown sugar (2 2/3 oz)
  • 3/4 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/8 tsp table salt
  • 8 tbsp unsalted butter (1 stick, 4 oz), melted and still warm
  • 1 3/4 cups cake flour (7 oz)

Cake

  • 1 1/4 cups cake flour (5 oz)
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar (3 1/2 oz)
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp table salt
  • 6 tbsp unsalted butter (3 oz), cut into 6 pieces, softened but still cool
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 1 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • 1/3 cup liquid buttermilk
  • Confectioners’ sugar, for dusting

Instructions

  1. Make the crumb topping: In a medium bowl, whisk together granulated sugar, dark brown sugar, cinnamon, and salt. Stir in the melted butter until combined.
  2. Add the cake flour and stir with a rubber spatula or wooden spoon until the mixture forms a thick, cohesive dough. Set aside to cool to room temperature, 10–15 minutes.
  3. Prepare the pan: Adjust oven rack to upper-middle position and heat oven to 325°F. Cut a 16-inch length of parchment or foil and fold lengthwise to 7 inches wide. Spray an 8-inch square baking dish with nonstick spray. Fit the parchment/foil sling into the dish, pushing it into the corners and up the sides; allow overhang for easy removal. Spray the sling with nonstick spray as well.
  4. Make the cake batter: In a large bowl, combine cake flour, granulated sugar, baking soda, and salt. Add the softened butter pieces and beat on low speed until the mixture resembles moist crumbs and no large butter pieces remain.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk together egg, egg yolk, vanilla, and buttermilk.
  6. With the mixer running on low, pour the egg mixture into the flour mixture and beat until just combined, then increase speed to medium and beat until light and fluffy, about 1 minute.
  7. Pour the batter into the prepared baking dish and spread evenly.
  8. Add the crumbs: Break the crumb topping dough into large pea- to marble-sized chunks and sprinkle evenly over the batter, covering the entire surface.
  9. Bake at 325°F for 35–40 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cake comes out clean (crumbs on top will remain soft).
  10. Remove pan from oven and cool on a wire rack for at least 30 minutes.
  11. Use the parchment/foil sling to lift the cake from the pan. Dust with confectioners’ sugar just before serving.
