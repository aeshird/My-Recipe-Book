Sesame Noodles with Broccoli and Almonds

Notes

  • Prep: 6 min | Cook: 14 min | Total: 20 min
  • Yield: 6–8 servings

Ingredients

  • 1 pound (16 ounces) uncooked linguine (or any noodles, such as rice noodles, soba noodles, lo mein noodles, etc.)
  • 1 tablespoon peanut or vegetable oil
  • 3 to 4 cups chopped broccoli florets (about 1 medium head of broccoli)
  • 4 cloves garlic, peeled and thinly sliced
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1/2 cup chopped Blue Diamond Wasabi and Soy Sauce Almonds (optional)
  • 1 batch Sesame-Soy Vinaigrette (see below)
  • Thinly sliced green onions, toasted sesame seeds, coarsely ground black pepper, and crushed red pepper flakes (optional garnishes)

Sesame-Soy Vinaigrette

  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons rice wine vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon freshly cracked black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/2 teaspoon sriracha or hot chili oil

Instructions

  1. Cook the noodles al dente in a large stockpot of salted water according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Meanwhile, as the pasta cooks, heat the oil in a large sauté pan over medium-high heat. Add the broccoli florets and season with a few pinches of salt and pepper. Sauté, stirring once a minute or so, for 4–5 minutes, until slightly charred around the edges and cooked through.
  3. Stir in the garlic and continue sautéing for 1–2 minutes, stirring frequently, until the garlic is cooked and fragrant. Remove from the heat.
  4. In a small bowl or jar, whisk or shake together all of the sesame-soy vinaigrette ingredients until well combined.
  5. In a large mixing bowl, combine the cooked noodles, broccoli and garlic mixture, chopped almonds, and vinaigrette. Toss until everything is evenly coated.
  6. Serve warm or at room temperature, garnished with green onions, sesame seeds, black pepper, and red pepper flakes if desired.
