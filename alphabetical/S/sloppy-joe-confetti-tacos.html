Sloppy Joe Confetti Tacos

Notes

  • Prep: 20 min | Total: 20 min
  • Yield: 6 servings (2 tacos each)

Ingredients

  • 1 pound extra-lean (at least 90%) ground beef
  • 1 box (4.6 oz) taco shells (12 shells)
  • 1 can (15.5 oz) sloppy joe sauce
  • 1 small red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 can (11 oz) whole kernel sweet corn, drained
  • 1 can (2 1/4 oz) sliced ripe olives, drained
  • 1 cup thinly sliced romaine lettuce
  • 1/4 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup shredded reduced-fat sharp Cheddar cheese

Instructions

  1. Heat the oven to 350°F. In a 10-inch skillet, cook the ground beef over medium-high heat for 5–7 minutes, stirring frequently, until thoroughly cooked. Drain if necessary.
  2. Meanwhile, heat the taco shells as directed on the box.
  3. Stir the sloppy joe sauce, chopped bell pepper, and corn into the cooked beef. Cook for 2–3 minutes longer, stirring occasionally, until the mixture is hot and bubbly.
  4. Spoon about 1/4 cup of the beef mixture into each warm taco shell. Top with olives, lettuce, mozzarella, and Cheddar cheese.
  5. Serve immediately. (Flat-bottomed taco shells work well for easier filling and a little extra room.)
