Spaghetti Puttanesca

Notes

  • Cook Time: 10–30 min
  • Yield: 6 servings

Garlic Ciabatta (Optional)

  • 2 tablespoons butter, softened
  • 2 large garlic cloves, finely grated
  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped flatleaf parsley
  • 1 ounce (30 g) Parmesan, finely grated
  • 1 ready-to-bake ciabatta, cut into 12 thick slices

Spaghetti Puttanesca

  • 3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 red onion, finely chopped
  • 6 garlic cloves, finely sliced
  • 1/2 to 1 teaspoon dried chili flakes
  • 12 anchovy fillets in oil, drained and roughly chopped
  • 2 tablespoons baby capers, drained
  • 3 1/2 ounces pitted black olives, sliced
  • 1 pound 12 ounces cherry tomatoes (about 1 pound 5 ounces halved, 7 ounces quartered)
  • 1 pound 2 ounces dried spaghetti
  • Handful of flatleaf parsley, finely chopped
  • Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper

To Serve

  • Freshly grated Parmesan cheese

Instructions

  1. If serving garlic ciabatta, preheat the oven to 200°C/180°C fan (Gas 6) and line a baking tray with baking parchment. In a small bowl, mix the softened butter, grated garlic, parsley, and Parmesan together. Season lightly with salt and pepper.
  2. Lay the ciabatta slices on the prepared baking tray and spread liberally with the garlic butter. Bake on the top shelf of the oven for 12–14 minutes, or until golden brown. Set aside.
  3. Meanwhile, heat the olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chopped red onion and a pinch of salt. Cook for 5–7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened.
  4. Add the sliced garlic and chili flakes. Cook for 1–2 minutes, stirring frequently, until fragrant.
  5. Stir in the anchovies, capers, and olives. Cook for another 2–3 minutes, gently breaking up the anchovies so they melt into the sauce.
  6. Add the cherry tomatoes and season with a little salt and pepper. Simmer for 10–15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the tomatoes have broken down and the sauce has thickened.
  7. While the sauce simmers, cook the spaghetti in a large pot of salted boiling water according to the package directions until al dente. Reserve about 1/2 cup of the pasta cooking water, then drain.
  8. Add the cooked spaghetti to the puttanesca sauce, along with a splash of the reserved cooking water if needed to loosen. Toss well to coat the pasta in the sauce.
  9. Stir through the chopped parsley and adjust seasoning to taste.
  10. Serve the spaghetti hot, topped with freshly grated Parmesan and the garlic ciabatta on the side.
