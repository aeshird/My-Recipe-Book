Honey Garlic Mahi Tacos

Notes

  • Yield: 4 servings

Mahi & Marinade

  • 2 tbsp raw honey
  • 1 1/2 tbsp taco seasoning
  • 1 1/2 tsp minced garlic
  • 1 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1/4 tsp sea salt
  • 10 oz uncooked mahi mahi
  • 8 corn tortillas (6-inch)

Cilantro-Lime Rice

  • 1/2 cup cooked brown rice (about 3 tbsp uncooked)
  • 1 cup cauliflower rice, raw or frozen
  • 1 tsp olive oil
  • 1/2 tbsp lime juice
  • Pinch of lime zest
  • 1 tbsp cilantro, chopped

Toppings

  • 1 cup chopped green cabbage
  • 4 tbsp Cashew Sour Cream (see Food Prep Guide)
  • 4 tbsp salsa
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, mix honey, taco seasoning, minced garlic, melted coconut oil, and sea salt.
  2. Add mahi mahi to the bowl and toss until well coated. Let marinate while preparing the rice.
  3. Cook the brown rice and cauliflower rice according to package directions. Once fully cooked, combine the two in a bowl.
  4. Stir in olive oil, lime juice, lime zest, and chopped cilantro. Weigh the rice mixture and divide by four to determine the amount needed for one serving. Keep warm.
  5. Cook the marinated mahi in a skillet over medium heat or on a grill until cooked through and flaky, about 3â€“4 minutes per side, depending on thickness. Remove from heat and flake into bite-size pieces.
  6. Warm the corn tortillas in a dry skillet or microwave.
  7. To assemble each taco, add a portion of cilantro-lime rice to a tortilla, top with mahi, chopped cabbage, a spoonful of Cashew Sour Cream, and salsa.
  8. Serve with lime wedges for squeezing over the tacos.
