Enchiladas (From Penny Guler)

Meat Filling Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground chuck
  • 1 clove garlic, finely chopped
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp tequila, cognac, or water
  • 1 tbsp chili powder
  • 1 can (1 lb) kidney beans, undrained

Tomato Sauce Ingredients

  • 3 tbsp olive or salad oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup chopped onion
  • 1/4 cup chopped green pepper
  • 1 can (2 lb 3 oz) Italian tomatoes, undrained (or Italian sauce)
  • 1 can (6 oz) tomato paste
  • 1 beef bouillon cube
  • 3/4 cup boiling water
  • 2–3 tbsp finely chopped canned green chilies (or 4 oz can, drained)
  • Dash ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/8 tsp pepper
  • 1 cup grated sharp cheddar cheese (or more, to taste)

Instructions (Meat Filling)

  1. In a medium skillet over low heat, sauté the ground chuck, stirring with the garlic, salt, tequila (or cognac or water), and chili powder.
  2. Cook until the meat is browned. Drain off excess fat if necessary.
  3. Stir in the undrained kidney beans. Remove from heat and set aside.

Instructions (Tomato Sauce & Assembly)

  1. In a large saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add garlic, onion, and green pepper and cook until softened.
  2. Add Italian tomatoes, tomato paste, bouillon cube, and boiling water. Stir to combine.
  3. Add green chilies, cumin, salt, and pepper. Simmer until slightly thickened and flavors have blended.
  4. Fill tortillas with the meat mixture, roll up, and place seam-side down in a baking dish.
  5. Pour tomato sauce over the enchiladas and sprinkle generously with grated cheddar cheese.
  6. Bake until heated through and cheese is melted and bubbly (baking time and temperature can match your usual enchilada method).
